Recent advances in AI have been driven by rapid scaling of data, models, and compute—but at growing
energy, resource, and environmental costs that few labs can afford. This workshop focuses on the
**sustainability of AI systems** across their lifecycle, emphasizing data-, compute-, and energy-efficient
methods, as well as quantitative, reproducible metrics and benchmarks.

We invite contributions on methods, metrics, and real-world applications that enable AI systems to be
both **powerful and sustainable**.

### Topics of Interest

- **Data- and label-efficient learning** under resource constraints, including theoretical analyses and empirical studies
- Leveraging underutilized or unconventional **data sources** to reduce data and compute demands
- **High-performance computing ML pipelines**, including energy-, resource-, hardware-, and time-efficient perception and inference
- **Efficient multimodal AI** (e.g., adaptive modality selection, sensor scheduling, shared representations)
- Sustainable **re-use, adaptation, and life-cycle management** of ML models: fine-tuning of foundation models, in-context and test-time learning, continual learning, federated learning
- **Metrics, methodologies, and tools** for measuring efficiency and sustainability (budgeted performance, carbon accounting, standardized reporting, benchmarks)
- **Edge and embedded deployments**: design, deployment, and evaluation of efficient models in industrial, agricultural, mobility, healthcare, and other real-world environments
- **Sustainable hardware and materials**: algorithm–hardware co-design, low-power accelerators, environmentally responsible hardware life-cycles
- **Ethical, socio-economic, and socio-cultural aspects** of sustainable AI: equity of access, environmental justice, governance, and policy implications

## Submission Tracks

#### Full Papers *(Archival — PMLR Proceedings)*
- Up to **7 pages** for main content + up to **2 pages** for references (9 pages total)
- Accepted papers will be published in a dedicated volume of the **Proceedings of Machine Learning Research (PMLR)**
- Criteria: novelty, technical soundness, potential impact, clarity

#### Short Papers *(Non-Archival)*
- Up to **4 pages** + **1 page** for references (5 pages total)
- Intended for work-in-progress, preliminary results, and ideas benefiting from feedback
- Non-archival: authors may later submit extended versions elsewhere

#### Position Papers *(Non-Archival)*
- Up to **2 pages** + **1 page** for references (3 pages total)
- For research visions, open challenges, benchmark proposals, or critical perspectives
- Primarily serve as discussion starters at the workshop

> All submissions must follow the **official IJCAI workshop formatting guidelines** and will undergo **double-blind peer review**.
